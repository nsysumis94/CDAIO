02 · Selection Methodology

Stakeholder Power–Interest Matrix

Each candidate was scored on two dimensions derived from their profile descriptions. Selection priority was determined by quadrant placement and strategic role on the council.

Scoring Dimensions

Influence (Power) Score 1–5:
Does this person have approval/veto authority? Do they control budgets, platforms, or pipelines? Are they a key node in NexaMind's decision chain? Can they mobilize or block others?

Interest Score 1–5:
Is this person directly impacted by governance outcomes? Do they actively engage with compliance, delivery risk, or client trust issues? Do they have a personal or professional stake in the result?

Quadrant → Council Strategy

The framework determines why each candidate should be included or excluded, and how to engage them on the council. High-Influence members need to be inside the tent — not managed from outside.

The critical insight for this simulation: governance must be designed for the people who will actually execute it — not just those with formal authority.

06 · Council Balance Analysis

Composition & Tension Management

The selected 7 deliberately span NexaMind's four organizational dynamics — ensuring no single faction can dominate, and that governance reaches all the way down to execution.

Influence Score Distribution

Organizational Faction Coverage

Four factions are represented — Growth Engine, Governance Core, Technical Bridge, and Execution Reality. No single faction holds a majority (2-2-2-1). The critical design insight from the simulation: governance that does not reach Execution Reality will be ignored regardless of how well it is designed at the top.
KT
Execution Reality — Why This Faction Cannot Be Left Out

The simulation explicitly states: "Kevin Tan and Emily Zhang represent execution reality, where informal practices determine success more than formal policies." This is a distinct organizational layer — separate from the Technical Bridge (Wu, Chen) who operate at the architecture and knowledge level.

Kevin Tan's informal power is the specific kind that governance frameworks most commonly fail to account for: he controls what actually gets deployed, not just what is approved. A policy that Wu designs and Bennett approves can still be bypassed if Tan's pipelines do not enforce it. His inclusion closes this gap.

Emily Zhang (not selected) also belongs to this faction — but she operates at peer/cultural influence level, not pipeline control. She is better placed as a frontline governance champion outside the council.
07 · First Decisive Action

Implement the Client Data Segregation Protocol (CDSP)

Within 30 days of council formation, mandate a technical and process-level firewall between client data assets. No model, dataset, or analytical framework derived from a client engagement may be reused across another engagement without explicit council-approved authorization.

This action is chosen because it addresses all three mandate dimensions simultaneously: it protects client confidentiality directly, creates a standardized governance touchpoint, and — critically — it is enforced at the pipeline level by Kevin Tan, making it impossible to bypass without triggering an automated alert. Governance that lives in systems is harder to ignore than governance that lives in policy documents.

Target: Protection of client trust and confidentiality · Completion window: 90 days

08 · Strategic Rationale

Why This Council Composition Works

Three design principles drove every inclusion and exclusion decision.

🏛️

Authority Without Blind Spots

The council covers all decision levers: executive mandate (Grant), legal veto (Bennett), technical platform (Wu), and operational pipeline (Tan). No decision can be made without someone with real power in the room — and no power goes unrepresented.

⚙️

Governance Where It Actually Happens

Kevin Tan (Data Engineering) and Victor Chen (Knowledge Management) represent the execution layer where reuse actually occurs. Without them, governance remains theoretical. Their inclusion converts informal power into formal accountability.

⚖️

Converting Resistors into Champions

Sophie Laurent is included specifically because she resists governance. High-Influence resistors outside the council will undermine it. Inside the council, she becomes co-responsible for governance outcomes — and her delivery expertise makes the governance more practical.
